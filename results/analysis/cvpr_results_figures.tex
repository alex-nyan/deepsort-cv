\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\paragraph{Experimental setup.}
We evaluate four tracker variants on MOTChallenge-format sequences:
\textbf{Baseline} (constant-velocity Kalman filter, no camera compensation),
\textbf{Accel-only} (constant-acceleration Kalman filter),
\textbf{CMC-only} (constant velocity with ECC-based camera-motion compensation),
and \textbf{Full} (constant acceleration with ECC).
We report \textbf{MOTA} and \textbf{IDF1} averaged over sequences, and \textbf{IDS} summed over sequences (fewer identity switches is better).
On SportsMOT soccer we pool \textbf{train and validation} splits (30 sequences total); on SoccerNet Tracking we evaluate the \textbf{train} split (57 sequences).
BoT-SORT baselines are omitted here; SportsMOT sequences without detections were filled using YOLOv8, whereas SoccerNet uses released detections.

\paragraph{Tracking performance.}
Figure~\ref{fig:ablation_mota_idf1} shows mean MOTA and IDF1 per sequence.
On \textbf{SportsMOT}, enabling camera compensation yields the largest gains: \textbf{CMC-only} and \textbf{Full} reach similar MOTA ($\approx$0.78), clearly above Baseline ($\approx$0.73).
IDF1 follows the same trend, with CMC-only slightly strongest among ECC variants.
On \textbf{SoccerNet train}, \textbf{CMC-only} is best overall (MOTA $\approx$0.95, IDF1 $\approx$0.74).
Adding acceleration on top of ECC (\textbf{Full}) does not improve headline metrics on this split; MOTA and especially IDF1 drop relative to CMC-only, while Accel-only remains a large improvement over Baseline.

Figure~\ref{fig:ablation_ids} reports the same trend through identity switches.
IDS decreases monotonically toward Full on SportsMOT; on SoccerNet, IDS is lowest for CMC-only and rises again for Full.
Together, Figs.~\ref{fig:ablation_mota_idf1}--\ref{fig:ablation_ids} indicate that \textbf{ECC is the dominant improvement under shared hyperparameters}, while \textbf{CA combined with ECC improves identity stability on SportsMOT but hurts it on SoccerNet train} in our configuration---suggesting dataset-dependent tuning rather than a universally optimal ``full'' stack.

\paragraph{Kalman prediction analysis.}
Figure~\ref{fig:prediction_horizon} compares open-loop center-error of constant-velocity versus constant-acceleration Kalman filters fit to ground-truth tracks, at prediction horizons $h \in \{1,5,10,15,20\}$ frames (see supplement for protocol details).
\textbf{CA achieves lower error than CV at $h{=}1$} on both datasets, but \textbf{error grows faster for CA at longer horizons}, surpassing CV by $h{\geq}5$.
This behavior is expected when forecasting without measurements: extra acceleration state helps one-step prediction but amplifies drift over many steps.
We emphasize that Fig.~\ref{fig:prediction_horizon} is \emph{not} contradictory with tracker metrics---association uses frequent measurement updates---but it clarifies where CA does and does not show advantage.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
SportsMOT relies on generated detections where missing; SoccerNet uses dataset-provided detections.
We do not report SoccerNet test/challenge splits here.
For benchmark-aligned SportsMOT headline numbers, readers may prefer validation- or test-only evaluation; our pooled train+val evaluation is kept for stable per-variant comparison on a fixed set of sequences.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig1_ablation_mota_idf1.png}
  \caption{\textbf{Four-way ablation (tracking).}
  Mean MOTA and IDF1 per sequence on SportsMOT soccer (train+val, 30 seq.; \emph{left}) and SoccerNet Tracking train (57 seq.; \emph{right}).
  Camera-motion compensation (ECC) improves both metrics on both datasets.
  Combining constant-acceleration KF with ECC (\textbf{Full}) matches strong ECC performance on SportsMOT but underperforms \textbf{CMC-only} on SoccerNet train under shared hyperparameters.}
  \label{fig:ablation_mota_idf1}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.92\linewidth]{fig2_ablation_ids_totals.png}
  \caption{\textbf{Identity switches by configuration.}
  Total IDS summed over all sequences (lower is better).
  SportsMOT (\emph{green}) benefits monotonically through \textbf{Full}.
  SoccerNet train (\emph{red}) is best at \textbf{CMC-only}; \textbf{Full} increases IDS relative to \textbf{CMC-only}, consistent with lower IDF1 in Fig.~\ref{fig:ablation_mota_idf1}.}
  \label{fig:ablation_ids}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig3_prediction_horizon.png}
  \caption{\textbf{Open-loop Kalman prediction error vs.\ horizon.}
  Mean pixel error for CV (8D) vs.\ CA (12D) filters fit to GT tracks on SportsMOT soccer (\emph{left}) and SoccerNet train (\emph{right}).
  CA is better at horizon 1 frame but degrades faster without measurements; long-horizon curves should not be equated with tracker association performance.}
  \label{fig:prediction_horizon}
\end{figure}
